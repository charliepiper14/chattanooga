Frequency 5.83x is past every action threshold

Flag level is 3.0x. Action level is 4.0x. The campaign is now at 5.83x. The average person in the target audience saw a CBC ad nearly six times in the last seven days. CPMs rose 8.4% and CTR fell 15.5% in the same window. This is textbook fatigue, not a creative quality issue.

The signal that confirms it: AOV held

Average order value actually rose to $54.71 from $51.67. The customers who did buy spent more. That rules out an offer or pricing problem. The shrinkage was at the top of the funnel: fewer impressions converted to clicks, fewer clicks converted to carts. That is a creative saturation pattern.

STATIC 3 cooled, FOUNDER TALK paused

Two of the three ads carrying spend last week stopped contributing. STATIC 3 dropped from 5.69x to 1.25x as residual spend, then was paused. FOUNDER TALK was paused mid-week at 3.67x frequency. The pipeline did exactly what it was supposed to do, but the replacements need a few more days to ramp.

The Obsidian Scale batch is the floor

Four new Obsidian-themed ads went live this week. Combined they spent $68.97, generated 5 purchases and $281.77 in revenue. Blended ROAS 4.09x. Frequency averaging 2.17x. These are the ads that prevented this week from being a 1.5x story. The architecture is working.

11: Diagnosis

What's Actually Wrong

Creative concentration risk

For three weeks the campaign rode two ads: STATIC 3 in Obsidian, and Founder Talk in Adv+ Winners. Both came offline this week. The new Obsydian Scale batch is healthy but small. The Adv+ Winners side is now leaning entirely on Founder Unbox at 4.17x frequency. The portfolio is too thin.

Frequency saturation hit the campaign

5.83x campaign frequency. CPMs up 8.4%. CTR down 15.5%. CPP up 28.7%. Every signal points to audience overexposure on the existing creative pool. This is exactly the pattern flagged in the Apr 14 deck and the Apr 27 crib sheet. The fix is fresh creative, not budget changes.

New creative pipeline timing was tight

The Obsydian Scale ads went live mid-week. They needed a few more days of runway to fully replace the spend coming off STATIC 3 and Founder Talk. The handover from old hero ads to new ones happened, but with a small gap that compressed performance for 3 to 4 days.

What is not wrong

AOV held at $54.71. Blended MER above 9x. New customer acquisition steady at 18 per week. CAC stable at $13 to $15 blended. The product, the offer, the website, and the customer base are all fine. This is purely a creative-rotation timing issue, not a brand or business problem.

14: Budget & Scaling

The Scaling Window

Hold spend at $39 daily until ROAS clears 3.5x for 3 consecutive days.

Last week we cleared the threshold and triggered a small Obsidian budget increase. This week the threshold is broken on Meta numbers. The standing rule applies in reverse: do not scale into a fatigue cycle. Spend stays flat through the creative refresh, then the scaling conversation reopens once two new ads sustain above 4x for 3 to 4 days. The MER is still 9.68x so the business is profitable. The Meta side just needs creative oxygen.

Why we don't pull spend

Pulling spend now compounds the problem. Lower spend means less data, longer learning phases, slower recovery for the new ads. Holding at $39 daily lets the new Obsydian Scale batch get the impressions it needs to lock in performance. The right move is creative refresh, not budget surgery.
