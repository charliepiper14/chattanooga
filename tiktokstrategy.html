TikTok is Not Meta

Before we get into it: TikTok's algorithm, learning phase, and creative requirements work differently than Meta. This is what it means for CBC.

How TikTok Differs

Learning Phase is Hungrier

TikTok needs 50 conversions per ad group per week to exit learning. At $41 AOV, that means roughly $2,050 in revenue per ad group weekly. This shapes everything about how we structure campaigns. We consolidate instead of fragmenting.

Creative Fatigue is Faster

TikTok ads fatigue in 7-14 days compared to 3-4 weeks on Meta. You need fresh hooks constantly. The good news is that lo-fi content is faster to produce and actually performs better here.

Sound is Not Optional

93% of TikTok users watch with sound on, versus maybe 15% on Meta feed. Voiceover, music, and natural audio are required here, not nice-to-have.

TikTok Benchmarks for E-commerce

Average CPM $9 - $14
Good CTR >1.0%
Strong CTR >1.5%
Hook Rate (2-sec view) >25%
Hold Rate (full view) >8%
Average CVR (cold) 1.5% - 2.5%

The Math for CBC

At $41 AOV, 2% CVR, $12 CPM, the estimated CPA lands around $29. That's 1.4X ROAS before optimization. We need strong creative and proper structure to hit 2X+ in test phase.

About Your Question on Repurposing Meta Ads

The video content can transfer, but the format needs adaptation. Your BTS packing videos and Todd's founder content are exactly the kind of stuff that wins on TikTok. Authentic, personality-driven, real. But we need to re-cut with stronger hooks in the first 1-2 seconds, add native elements like text overlays and trending sounds, make sure everything is vertical 9:16, and ideally post organically first before boosting as Spark Ads. Your static images won't work here. TikTok is video-only for performance.

03 // Prospecting Structure

Consolidated Architecture

TikTok's learning phase demands consolidation. ONE campaign with TWO ad groups max so each gets enough conversion volume to optimize properly.

The Math Behind This Structure

At $100/day budget and roughly $25 target CPA, we expect around 4 conversions/day which is 28/week. Splitting across 4+ ad groups like you might on Meta would starve each one. Two ad groups at $50/day each gives us the best shot at 25+ conversions per ad group weekly, close enough to exit learning. *Budget figures shown are illustrative examples to demonstrate the approach, not committed amounts.

Why Broad Works

TikTok's algorithm is exceptionally good at finding buyers from broad audiences. Interest targeting often restricts reach without improving efficiency. Start broad and let the pixel learn.

When to Add Interests

Only after Week 4 if Broad plateaus or if LAL outperforms significantly. Test one interest stack: Men's Grooming + Outdoor + DIY/Tradesman. Keep as separate ad group.

Audience Exclusions

Exclude purchasers from the past 180 days, website visitors if retargeting separately, and TikTok profile followers to avoid paying for organic reach.

Smart Performance Campaign Option

TikTok's Smart Performance Campaign (SPC) is their version of Meta's Advantage+. Fully automated targeting and creative optimization.

When to consider: After 200+ conversions when pixel has strong data. Or as a parallel test in Week 5+.

Not for launch: New pixels need manual structure to build signal first. SPC works better with mature data.

04 // Retargeting Structure

Full-Funnel Retargeting

Retargeting on TikTok is where margins live. We launch retargeting in Week 3 once we have audience pools built from prospecting traffic.

Why Wait Until Week 3?

Retargeting audiences need volume. At $100/day prospecting, we'll have around 3,000-5,000 site visitors by Week 3. That's enough to run retargeting without frequency issues. Launching retargeting Day 1 with no pixel data means wasted spend.

Testing Protocol

  • 1. Same body, different hooks to isolate hook performance. Don't change multiple variables at once.
  • 2. 4-6 ads per ad group for enough variety for algorithm to optimize, but not so many it fragments spend.
  • 3. $15-20 spend = kill decision so you don't let losers drain budget. Cut fast.
  • 4. Winners get duplicated by taking winning hook and applying to new body content.
  • 5. Refresh every 10-14 days because even winners fatigue. Plan for iteration.

Use TikTok Creative Center

Research top ads at ads.tiktok.com/business/creativecenter. Filter by Personal Care, US, last 7 days. Analyze hooks, pacing, CTAs. Free competitive intelligence.

Landing Page Consideration

TikTok traffic is colder and more impulsive than Meta. Expect higher bounce rate (60-70%), shorter session duration, and almost all mobile. Make sure your landing page loads in under 3 seconds, has sticky add-to-cart on mobile, shows social proof above fold, and has clear trust signals like Made in USA and money-back guarantee. Consider a TikTok-specific landing page if conversion rate is significantly lower than Meta.

06 // Measurement Setup

Attribution Stack

TikTok's native attribution is directionally useful but not gospel. We'll triangulate with Triple Whale using the same approach you're already using for Meta.

TikTok Events API (Required)

Browser pixel alone misses 30-40% of conversions post-iOS14.5. Events API sends server-side data directly to TikTok. This is mandatory.

Setup: Shopify then Settings then Apps then TikTok then Enable Events API. Takes 10 minutes. Verify in Events Manager showing "Active".

Triple Whale Integration

Add TikTok as a source in Triple Whale for unified view. Triple Whale's first-party pixel catches conversions TikTok misses.

Expectation: Triple Whale will likely show 20-40% more TikTok conversions than TikTok reports natively, similar to the Meta discrepancy you've seen.

Attribution Windows

Window Setting Why
Click-through 7-day Standard for e-commerce
View-through 1-day TikTok VTA over-attributes so keep tight
Engaged View 6-sec, ON TikTok-specific, captures intent

Events to Verify

  • ✓ ViewContent for product page views
  • ✓ AddToCart for cart additions
  • ✓ InitiateCheckout for checkout starts
  • ✓ CompletePayment for purchases (optimize here)

Use TikTok Pixel Helper Chrome extension to verify all events fire correctly. Do a test purchase.

Post-Purchase Survey

Add "How did you hear about us?" to checkout with "TikTok" as an option. Zero-party attribution data validates platform-reported conversions. This is especially valuable for TikTok where view-through attribution is murky. Cross-reference survey responses with TikTok and Triple Whale data monthly.

07 // Scaling Protocol

How to Scale Winners

Scaling on TikTok is more sensitive than Meta. Aggressive budget increases reset learning. This is the playbook for sustainable growth.

Vertical Scaling (Same Ad Group)

The 20-30% Rule

Increase budget by max 20-30% every 48-72 hours. Larger jumps reset learning phase and spike CPA temporarily.

If CPA below $20 and stable for 3+ days
Scale Increase budget 20-30%
If CPA $20-28 and ROAS above 2X
Hold Maintain, monitor 48hrs
If CPA above $30 for 48+ hours
Cut Reduce budget 30% or pause

Horizontal Scaling (Duplication)

When to Duplicate

When vertical scaling hits a ceiling and CPA rises with each increase, duplicate the winning ad group with fresh budget. New ad group starts fresh learning.

Duplication Protocol

  • 1. Duplicate winning ad group exactly
  • 2. Set budget at original level ($50/day)
  • 3. Let it run 48hrs before judging
  • 4. If duplicate matches original, you've found scale ceiling
  • 5. If duplicate fails, original had audience-specific magic

08 // Diagnostic Framework

When Things Aren't Working

A systematic troubleshooting guide. If performance drops, here's what to check and in what order.

Problem: High CPM (above $15)

Check:

  • → Audience too narrow? Expand or go broad.
  • → Ad frequency too high? Check 7-day frequency.
  • → Seasonal competition? CPMs spike during holidays.
  • → Low relevance score? Creative isn't resonating.

Fix: Broaden targeting, refresh creative, check ad quality.

Problem: Low CTR (below 0.8%)

Check:

  • → Hook rate okay but CTR low? Body content issue.
  • → Hook rate also low? Opening isn't stopping scroll.
  • → CTA clear? Users don't know what to do.
  • → Audience mismatch? Content not resonating with target.

Fix: Test new hooks, stronger CTA, check audience alignment.

Problem: High CPA (above $35)

Check:

  • → CTR good but CPA high? Landing page issue.
  • → Check page speed (must be under 3s).
  • → Mobile experience broken? Test on phone.
  • → Pixel firing correctly? Verify in Events Manager.
  • → Check Triple Whale. Is TikTok under-reporting?

Fix: Audit landing page, verify tracking, check true CPA in Triple Whale.

Problem: Sudden Performance Drop

Check:

  • → Creative fatigue? Check frequency and days running.
  • → Budget change? Recent scaling can reset learning.
  • → Audience exhaustion? LAL may have peaked.
  • → Platform changes? TikTok algorithm updates happen.
  • → Competitor activity? Increased auction competition.

Fix: Refresh creative, test new audiences, check for external factors.

The 72-Hour Rule

Before making major changes like killing campaigns or massive budget cuts, wait 72 hours of consistent data. TikTok has high day-to-day variance. One bad day isn't a trend. Three bad days is a signal. Exception: If Hook Rate is below 15% or CTR is below 0.5% after $20 spend, kill immediately because that creative won't recover.

Weekly Health Check

Every Monday, review: Frequency by ad group where anything above 6 needs attention, creative performance to see if any winners are fatiguing, audience overlap to see if ad groups are competing, attribution gap between TikTok and Triple Whale, and blended MER to see TikTok's impact on overall business.
